TAKE URGENT ACTION TO COMBAT CLIMATE CHANGE AND ITS IMPACTS

Sir David Attenborough’s statement that “We are facing a man-made disaster on a global scale. Our greatest threat in thousands of years. Climate change.” reflects the urgency captured in Sustainable Development Goal 13: Climate Action. SDG 13 calls for rapid reductions in emissions, stronger climate resilience, and the integration of climate policy across all levels of planning. Attenborough’s quote emphasizes that climate change is not a distant or natural crisis, but one driven by human activity particularly industrial pollution and unsustainable practices. This alignment between the SDG and the quote underscores a shared message: climate change is happening now, and it is our responsibility to stop it.

WE MUST ACT URGENTLY AND DECISIVELY TO PROTECT OUR PLANET BEFORE THE DAMAGE BECOMES IRREVERSIBLE.

“The Spread” is an interactive generative artwork that visualizes how pollution infiltrates our environment. Using drifting smoke and slime mold like patterns, it reflects the slow and invasive spread of industrial emissions, resembling blood vessels within the Earth. Red scanning frames detect toxic agents, symbolizing climate instability and nature’s response. The work encourages viewers to reflect on the invisible forces shaping our world and inspires action, even the smallest, against climate change.

ACT I: THE SPREAD

“The Starved” with he skeletal centipede represents humanity, relentlessly crawling over a fragile world in its mechanical pursuit of profit. Each click is an act of exploitation that shatters the pristine environment, causing the background to bleed red and corrupting the white particles of life into black decay. Though the world regenerates, humanity's destructive cycle of greed is endless, always ready to exploit it anew.

act II: THE STARVED

“The Dekay” illustrating a sparkling butterfly slowly decaying, "The Dekay" borrows an image of an insect- representation of nature- portraying the collapse of biodiversity by the increasing threat of rising temperatures, habitat loss, and pollution. Instead of warning, this work provides a fictional vision (metaphorized by the eye-shaped cursor) of a slowly dying planet, where there no longer signs of living habitats; in order to emphasize the importance of taking action and saving Home.
