Climate change is not a distant or natural crisis, but one driven by human activity particularly industrial pollution and unsustainable practices for years. The significancy showed by climate action being one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals of the United Nation. Both the SDG and the quote underscores a shared message: climate change is happening now, and it is our responsibility to stop it.

To turn on the future, let's go through the flow of smoke and have a deeper look into the story...

"The Spread" is a generative art piece that depicts industrial smoke, with red pixel clusters growing along its edges and on the ground. These red pixels symbolize a destructive virus, representing the widespread damage of pollution. As the smoke drifts, the red patches on the earth grow larger, reflecting the pollution's impact on the ecosystem. The project visualizes the visible effects of pollution while reminding us of the serious threat of climate change, encouraging viewers to take action.

act I: The Spread

"The Pillar" is a generative artwork with the central image is factory smokestacks, rising like "pillars" of pollution, symbolizing the devastating impact of industrialization on the environment. Behind is "heartbeat of the Earth", representing the fragile and endangered life of the planet. The "skeletal centipede" represents humanity, relentlessly crawling over a fragile world in its mechanical pursuit of profit. The project not only visually depicts the problem of environmental pollution, but also conveys a strong message about the urgency of combating climate change and the serious consequences it brings.

act II: The Pillar

"The Factory" visualizing the vital problem of climate change, with the gas mask as a key visual elements, eyes replaced by mechanical gears spinning, the generative mini-gears and mechanical pipeline system running in the background; it symbolizes the industrial factory manufacturing progress that releasing wasted fumes into the air as blocks of smoke coming from the bottom. The idea is to represents the paradox of human: building infrastructures to enhance our living environment while poisoning it at the same time. We do not need a mask as a shield, as a cover or an escape; we need to value our breath with a solution from the root of every conscious decision.
